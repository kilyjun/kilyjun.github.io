Junhyeok Kil

I am an undergraduate research assistant at the Composites Manufacturing & Simulation Center at Purdue University. I work on scaling advanced machine learning methods to solve composite manufacturing problems. My current focus is on solving parametric problems in additive manufacturing applications.

I live by this credo.

Research

I am exploring the application of Physics-Informed Neural Networks (PINNs) to attain parametric solutions for various problems in composite manufacturing processes.

As part of the Data Mine Corporate Partners program, my team developed an application for Procter & Gamble that automates network meta-analysis (NMA) for product comparison. This tool allows users to efficiently compare products, saving time and resources by eliminating the need for numerous pairwise comparisons.

This research involves developing a physics-based framework that automates the prediction and shape compensation for 3D-printed tooling made from composite materials. The framework integrates various inputs to carry out thermomechanical analysis, helping manufacturers reduce waste and improve product performance.

Projects

I developed an application for real-time acquisition, processing, and visualization of LiDAR data.

I created a Python script that generates continuous single-line animations by using edge detection and spline fitting techniques. This project is a fusion of art and technology, producing unique visual outputs from various inputs.

I developed a geospatial dashboard that displays all past projects in an interactive format. This dashboard allows users to visualize project data across different geographical locations and analyze patterns over time.
